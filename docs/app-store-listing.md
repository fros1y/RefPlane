## Description

---

Underpaint turns your photo library into a painting studio.

Whether you're working from a landscape photograph, a portrait session, or a still-life setup, Underpaint helps you see your reference the way a painter sees it — in tone, value, color regions, depth, and paint.

—

STUDY MODES

Original — see your reference exactly as shot.

Tonal — converts the image to a perceptually accurate grayscale using the Rec. 709 luminance formula, the same standard used in professional video. Use this to study the light-and-shadow structure of your scene before you ever mix a color.

Value — reduces the image to a small number of discrete tonal bands (you choose how many). Seeing your scene as three, four, or five distinct values makes it easier to plan a painting with strong tonal structure.

Color — extracts the dominant color regions of your image using perceptual clustering. Each region is shown as a flat, simplified area so you can read the color composition of your scene at a glance.

—

PAINT RECIPES

In Color mode, Underpaint can decompose each region into an actual paint mixture recipe using Golden Heavy Body Acrylic colors.

The mixing model is based on Kubelka-Munk spectral theory — the same physical model used in professional color-matching software. Instead of blending RGB numbers, Underpaint simulates how real pigments absorb and scatter light, so the predicted mixtures are physically meaningful.

Choose from curated palette presets — Zorn, Primary, Warm, Cool, or the full library of 78 Golden Heavy Body colors. Each region shows the contributing pigments and their approximate mixing ratios.

—

DEPTH EFFECTS

Enable Depth Effects and an on-device depth estimation model separates your scene into foreground and background. Use the Background slider to set the depth cutoff, then choose how the background is treated:

Depth Effects — de-emphasizes the background with reduced contrast and muted color, like atmospheric perspective.
Blur — softens the background with Gaussian blur, simulating shallow depth of field. The blur can be dialed up for a strongly smoothed background.
Remove — replaces the background with a solid neutral tone, isolating your subject entirely.

Surface Contours — with depth on, overlay contour lines (isolines) that trace equal-depth curves across the scene, revealing the 3D form of your subject.

—

SIMPLIFY

The Simplify control runs your reference through an on-device super-resolution model that smooths texture and simplifies detail while keeping the major shapes intact. Use it to strip away photographic noise and see the underlying forms more clearly. A Strength slider lets you control how aggressively the image is simplified.

The Kuwahara filter gives your image a painterly, flat-color look by averaging local color while preserving edges. Dial from R1 (subtle) to R16 (aggressive) and see your photo as broad, paintable shapes.

—

GRID OVERLAY

Add a configurable reference grid to any view. Set the number of divisions, choose square cells or image-fit cells, add diagonals and center lines, and adjust line color and opacity. The grid helps you transfer proportions from reference to canvas.

—

COMPARE

Drag the compare handle to reveal a split-screen view of the original and processed image side by side. Use it to evaluate how much a mode or simplification changes the underlying reference.

—

EXPORT

Share the processed image directly from the app using the standard iOS share sheet. Send it to Files, save it to Photos, or open it in another app.

—

PRIVACY

Underpaint collects no personal data. The app uses no analytics, no tracking, and no accounts. Every image is processed entirely on your device. Nothing leaves your phone.

—

Underpaint is for oil painters, watercolorists, gouache painters, and illustrators who want to get more out of their reference photographs before they pick up a brush.
